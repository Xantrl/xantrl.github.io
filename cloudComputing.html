CLOUD COMPUTING

IS CHANGING THE WORLD

    Get Started
The advent of Cloud Computing has been changing not only in the Information Technology field but also in many aspects of the modern world. The invention of Internet and the rapid evolution of digital device together with Cloud Computing has made the world "flatter". Communication becomes easier, the way we work is more different. Improved connectivity and processing means enhanced collaboration, increased productivity. Cloud computing has, therefore, changed the business world by replacing the traditional on-site processes with high computing power with greater information and data access, promoting cooperation and sharing infrastructure for more efficiency. Moreover, it created a level field on the Internet for various industries and sectors, hence, the business world became more competitive, and competition means development. Cloud computing is undoubtedly one of the most significant pillar in the IT sector, throughout its history, it has grown to form a virtual globalization model and it will continue to evolve. As of right now, the rapid transition from the traditional IT systems to the cloud systems is on the rise all over the world and in nearly all aspects of the modern society.

SERVERLESS MODEL

As more and more businesses beginning to find cloud migration attractive, partly due to the Covid-19 pandemic. The tangible benefits that the cloud has demonstrated will most likely means that businesses which have already moved their digital operations to the cloud may be reluctant to switch back or even abandon the idea of switching back to the traditional systems. And those who have not already done so will probably follow the same path of irreversible cloud migration.
A more advanced representation of the serverless architecture is the Internet connection to the cloud utilizing satellites, which enables high-speed Internet anywhere in the world. While the cloud computational capability is needed in various industries and in various environments, providing the access to the cloud is not always possible due to the lack of connections to the Internet, especially in rural areas where most agriculture and research activities occurs. The satellites provide the answer.
Modular datacenters that are connected to the Internet satellites can enable cloud computing in almost anywhere on Earth. An example is the recent partnership between Microsoft and SpaceX which allows the satellites to connect to Microsoftâ€™s cloud service, including the modular datacenter that enable cloud computing in isolated locations.
Such advanced breakthroughs may eventually restructure the entire landscape of the digital infrastructure of many sectors, making cloud computing the norm in the business and industrial world.

AUTOMATION WITH CLOUD

Another aspect that the cloud system may find itself useful is in the field of Internet of Things (IoT). The cloud can support highly distributed IoT network so that a large scale automated network of IoT devices can be efficiently connected with the help of AI and ML, which allows for highly efficient coordination among devices. Such automation represents the highest costs and time saving solutions for the future of industrial-scale automation.
The car industry are also taking notice of the possibilities that AI-powered vehicles can bring about as the technology has become matured enough to be in some degree of control over a vehicle. Such embedded technology with the utilization of the cloud and IoT sensors can help the vehicle navigate the traffic, communicate with other vehicles and provide feedback to the cloud to train the neural network. The potential that this breakthrough can produce may forever change the automobile industry in the near future.
The cloud also finds applications in the agriculture industry as a novel method of data collecting. IoT sensors combined with the connectivity enabled by the cloud provides valuable data regrading precipitation and other natural data that help optimize and reshape the production operations.
